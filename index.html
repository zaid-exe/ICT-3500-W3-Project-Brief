Optimize Your PC

Project Description

Optimize Your PC is a user-friendly website designed to provide tools, tips, and resources for Windows PC users who want to improve the performance, security, and longevity of their computers. It offers guides on maintenance, troubleshooting, and software recommendations in a way that is approachable for beginners and helpful for intermediate users. The most important part of this whole idea is that every bit of information will be verified, vetted, and have sources.

Business Needs

Many PC users face performance slowdowns, cluttered systems, and confusion about safe optimization practices. This project addresses the need for a trusted source of information that bridges the gap between overly technical guides and vague troubleshooting advice. By offering clear, step-by-step content, the site helps users avoid costly repairs, unnecessary purchases, or potential security issues. This site serves as a resource for power users who want to maximize their system's capabilities.

Goals

The primary goal is to empower users like myself and those with an interest in this hobby. Success from using this site properly comes in the form of post-optimized benchmarks of your system.

Use Cases

  • Me. I want a place where I can place all the information I've learned/gathered over the years.
  • My brother want to learn how to overclock his CPU within safe limits properly.
  • Anyone else who wants to learn about simple changes you can make to improve your system or network security.

Stakeholders

Primary stakeholders: Me
Secondary stakeholders: My brother.

Requirements

  • Simple, intuitive navigation that works across devices.
  • Responsive design to support desktops, tablets, and phones.
  • Clear instructions with screenshots and diagrams.
  • Accurate, regularly updated information to remain trustworthy.

Success Measures

Success will be tracked through the before-and-after performance benchmarks of one's system. In this case, my brother is being used as a guinea pig and will follow some of the tutorials I post.

Competitor

The only competitors are local PC shops that know what they're doing. This hobby isn't about making money, but rather for PC power users who love this kind of thing. Open information, collaboration, and knowledge are essential for this hobby. That's why a community for this hobby has existed, and will continue to grow.

Risks

  • Content becoming outdated as technology evolves rapidly.
  • Potential liability if third-party tools recommended later introduce issues.
  • Difficulty maintaining consistent updates without a clear content management plan. To combat this, dating everything is MANDATORY.
